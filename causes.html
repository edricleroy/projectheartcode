Causes of Histrionic Personality Disorder



Studies that have been done about histrionic and other personality disorders have identified several factors that may lead to the development of histrionic or other personality disorders:



Genetics

Genetics

Certain personality traits may be passed on to one by one's parents through inherited genes. These traits, also sometimes called the temperament, may make one more vulnerable to developing HPD.

Childhood Trauma

Children may cope with trauma, like child abuse or the death of a family member, which in the long run may be disruptive or problematic in their life and become part of a personality disorder.

When children are faced with a traumatic situation, they are more likely to get diagnosed with HPD, as their lives get disrupted by the events and they find themselves using negative ways to cope with it.

Parenting Styles

Children who experience parenting styles that lack boundaries, are over-indulgent or inconsistent may be more likely to develop histrionic personality disorder.
